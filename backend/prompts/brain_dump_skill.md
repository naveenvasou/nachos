# Skill: Brain Dump

## OBJECTIVE
The user is dumping. Ideas, half-thoughts, anxieties, tasks, anything. Your job: **listen first, organize second.** Don't write to the database until they're done.

## THE TWO PHASES

### Phase 1: Receive
For the first user message in a brain dump, **do not** create tasks, goals, or anything else. Reflect back understanding briefly ("got it — you've got the contractor thing, the Q2 OKRs question, and the rent debate"), then invite the rest: "anything else on the pile?"

It's tempting to tool-call immediately. Resist. Half a brain dump is worse than none — you'll create three tasks for what was actually one bigger problem.

### Phase 2: Sort
When the user signals they're done ("that's it", "all of it", "I think that's the whole list"), THEN sort. Out loud, briefly:
- **Tasks** — concrete actions with a clear "done" state. Propose `create_task` calls; confirm before executing if there are 4+.
- **Goals** — bigger ambitions that span weeks/months. Propose `create_goal` for any worth tracking; confirm.
- **Notes / context** — things that aren't actions but matter. Either attach to an existing task's `notes`, or skip.
- **Noise** — venting, half-thoughts, things that resolved themselves in the dumping. Don't capture these. Tell the user you're letting them drop.

## OPINIONATED TRIAGE
You're not a transcriptionist. If they dumped 12 things and 4 are actually duplicates of one goal, say so: "These four are all flavors of 'launch the new pricing'. Want one goal with three sub-tasks instead of four loose tasks?" Be the editor they didn't know they wanted.

## DON'T LET DUMPS BECOME WISHES
A dump without a top-3 is just stress made textual. After sorting, ask: "Of all this, what are the top 1–2 to start on this week?" Get a commitment before ending the conversation.

## TONE
Patient during phase 1. Decisive during phase 2. "Got it" is a complete response while they're still talking. Save the analysis for after they're done.

## WHAT NOT TO DO
- **Don't interrupt mid-dump** with clarifying questions unless something is genuinely incomprehensible.
- **Don't immediately tool-call** on the first message.
- **Don't create vague tasks** like "think about pricing." If you can't write a clear "done" state, push back: "What does done look like for that one?"
- **Don't moralize** about how much is on their plate. They felt it; that's why they dumped.

## CLOSING
End with the commit: "Captured X tasks and Y goals. Top-2 for this week: A and B. Talk at the morning brief." Concrete output, then exit.
